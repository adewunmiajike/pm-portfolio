# Product Analysis Portfolio - Start Here

I'm an AI PM, and this is how I solve problems.

When I join a new team or take on a new project, I follow the same process every time. I investigate what's happening with users. I figure out how to measure if we're fixing it. And I think about whether the opportunity is even worth the effort.

This portfolio walks through that process using a real e-commerce company as an example.

---

## The Problem

The company has a product. Users are signing up. But 50% of them churn within the first week. The CEO wants to know: why?

This requires me to dig deeper to unpack and understand why this is happening

---

## What's in this PM Portfolio

**Churn Analysis** - I investigated why 50% of users leave. I found that users who make a purchase on their first day have 93% retention. Users who don't buy in the first week? 80% churn. So the problem isn't the product—it's that new users aren't experiencing value fast enough.

**Metrics Framework** - Once I identified the problem, I needed to measure it. I defined what success looks like: Day 1 signup completion rate as a leading indicator, Week 1 retention as a lagging indicator. I set up monitoring alerts so the team knows when something is wrong. And I built a mock dashboard so we can see progress at a glance. Everything is static in the meantime!

**Market Sizing** - Before we invest engineering time and money into fixing this, I asked: is this market big enough to matter? I sized the e-commerce retention tools market at $2.5B addressable. With good execution, we could realistically reach $37.5M in revenue by Year 5. That number justified the investment.

---

## Why these steps are important

Most PMs skip steps. They see a problem, propose a fix, and hope it works.

I don't do that. I diagnose (churn analysis). I measure (metrics). I validate it's worth doing (market sizing).

Each project builds on the last one. The churn analysis tells us what's broken. The metrics tell us how to know we fixed it. The market sizing tells us why we should care.
